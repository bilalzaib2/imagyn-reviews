# IMAGYN PRODUCT MANIFESTO

---

# 1. Why IMAGYN Exists

Most Shopify apps are built by engineers, for a checkbox on a feature comparison page. They exist to be installed, not to be used well. The result is a storefront and a merchant dashboard made of a dozen different tools, each with its own visual language, its own settings sprawl, its own idea of what a button should look like. Merchants accept this because they've never been offered anything else.

We started IMAGYN because we don't accept it.

A review widget, a wishlist, a loyalty program — these are not complicated problems. They only look complicated because most software in this category is built without discipline. Every unnecessary setting, every inconsistent pattern, every cluttered screen is a small tax on a merchant's time and attention. Paid daily. Compounding for years.

Merchants deserve better software for the same reason any professional deserves better tools: because the quality of the tool determines the quality of the work, and the cost of the attention it steals. A merchant who spends less time fighting their apps has more time for their product, their customers, their business. That is the entire case for IMAGYN. Nothing about it is more complicated than that.

---

# 2. Our Mission

Give every Shopify merchant commerce tools considered enough, and quiet enough, that they never have to think about the tool — only about their business.

---

# 3. Our Vision

IMAGYN is not an app. It is an ecosystem.

A merchant should never feel like they've stitched together a storefront from unrelated parts. Reviews, wishlist, loyalty, referrals, forms, email, analytics — each should feel like a room in the same house, not a rented unit in a different building. One design language. One quality bar. One point of view about what commerce software should feel like.

We are building that house one room at a time. Reviews is the first. It will not be the last, and it will not be treated as more important than the ones that follow. Every product we ship makes a promise to the merchant: whatever we build next will feel like it belongs to the same company that built this.

---

# 4. Our Philosophy

**Software should reduce thinking, not require it.** Every decision a merchant has to make about how to use a tool is a decision they didn't have to make about their business. Good software absorbs complexity so the person using it doesn't have to carry it.

**Merchants should focus on their business, not on their software.** The moment a merchant is thinking about *how* our product works instead of *what they're trying to accomplish*, we have failed at our job, regardless of how many features we shipped.

**Beautiful software is easier to use.** This is not a matter of taste — it is a matter of function. Visual clarity is cognitive clarity. A well-designed screen tells you what matters before you've consciously read a word of it. An ugly screen makes you work to find that out.

**Simplicity requires discipline.** Simple is not the starting point of a product — it's what's left after everything unnecessary has been argued against and removed. It is harder to build than complexity, and it is the only version of the product we are willing to ship.

---

# 5. What We Believe

- Content comes first. The interface exists to serve the merchant's reviews, products, and customers — never to compete with them for attention.
- Interfaces should disappear. The best compliment a screen can receive is that no one remembers using it.
- Every click must have a purpose. If we can't state why a click exists, it doesn't.
- Calm is a competitive advantage. In a category full of noisy, alert-driven dashboards, quiet is a genuine product feature.
- Merchants deserve premium tools, not "good enough for a Shopify app."
- Defaults matter more than options. A great default serves everyone; a setting only serves the person who finds it.
- Speed is a design decision, not an engineering afterthought. A slow product feels untrustworthy before a merchant can articulate why.
- Consistency is kindness. A merchant who has learned one part of IMAGYN should already understand the rest.
- Restraint is a form of respect for the merchant's time and attention.
- We ship fewer things, done completely, rather than many things, done partially.

---

# 6. What We Will Never Build

**Feature bloat.** Every feature we add is a feature every merchant now has to navigate around, whether they use it or not. We will not chase feature-parity checklists with competitors who confuse "more" with "better."

**Cluttered dashboards.** A dashboard that tries to show everything succeeds at highlighting nothing. We will not solve a prioritization problem by displaying every option at equal visual weight.

**Endless settings.** A setting is an admission that we couldn't decide what the right behavior was. Every setting we add is a decision we're asking the merchant to make on our behalf. We will make that decision instead, and we will make it well.

**Interfaces that require training, documentation, or an onboarding tour to be usable.** If a product needs to be explained before it can be used, the product is the problem, not the merchant's unfamiliarity with it.

**Design that follows trends.** Trend-driven design ages within a year and signals that we were optimizing for how the product looks in a screenshot, not how it holds up in daily use. We design for permanence, not for a moment.

We say no to these things not because they're hard to build. Most of them are easy. We say no because they are easy ways to make a worse product, and we are not interested in the easy version of a worse product.

---

# 7. Product Principles

**One purpose per screen.** A screen that tries to do two jobs does neither well. If a screen needs a second purpose, it needs a second screen.

**Progressive disclosure.** Show what's needed now. Reveal complexity only when the merchant reaches for it, never before.

**Consistency beats novelty.** A clever new pattern that only exists in one corner of the product is a tax on everyone who encounters it for the first time. We reuse solved problems.

**Motion explains.** Animation exists to show a merchant *where something came from* and *where it's going* — never as decoration. If a motion doesn't clarify a state change, it doesn't ship.

**Typography creates hierarchy.** Before a merchant reads a single word, type size, weight, and spacing should have already told them what matters most on the screen.

**White space is functional.** Empty space is not wasted space — it's what allows the content that remains to be understood. Density is not a feature.

**Fast software feels trustworthy.** Latency reads as incompetence, even when the underlying work is sound. Performance is not a technical detail we tune at the end — it is part of what the merchant is judging us on from the first interaction.

---

# 8. Decision Framework

Before we build anything, we ask:

- Does this reduce cognitive load, or add to it?
- Does this help merchants complete their work faster?
- Would the product be better if we removed this instead of adding it?
- Would Apple ship this?
- Would Linear simplify it further?

If a feature cannot survive these five questions, it does not ship — regardless of how many merchants requested it, how easy it would be to build, or how good it would look on a roadmap slide.

---

# 9. The IMAGYN Experience

A merchant using IMAGYN should feel:

**Calm.** Nothing competes for their attention that doesn't deserve it.

**Confident.** The product never makes them guess whether an action worked, or what will happen next.

**Fast.** Every interaction responds as quickly as their own thinking.

**Focused.** The interface points at exactly one next step at a time.

**In control.** The product does the work; the merchant makes the decisions that actually matter.

This is not a mood board. It is a specification. Every screen we ship should be evaluated against these five words as rigorously as it's evaluated against any functional requirement.

---

# 10. The Long-Term Goal

IMAGYN becomes the design-first operating layer for Shopify commerce — an ecosystem, not a single app.

Reviews. Wishlist. Loyalty. Referrals. Forms. Email. Analytics. AI.

Each one solving a real, specific merchant problem. Each one built to the same standard. Each one sharing a single design language, a single quality bar, and a single point of view — so that a merchant who trusts us with one part of their storefront has every reason to trust us with the next.

We are not building a portfolio of apps that happen to share a logo. We are building one product, expressed across many surfaces.

Every product decision must protect the IMAGYN experience.
